Essential Compounds
Organic compounds and inorganic compounds are chemical compounds that make up the structures in cells. Organic compounds always contain Carbon and Hydrogen while inorganic compounds do not contain carbon and hydrogen together.
Lipids
A highly diverse group of fatty compounds made up mostly of hydrocarbons. They are part of our cell membranes and help control what goes in and out of our cells. They also help with absorbing vitamins, moving and storing energy, and making hormones.
Proteins
This compound is a critical component of all tissues and organs. They serve as structural support, biochemical catalysts, and initiators of cellular death. Proteins can also be hormones and enzymes.
