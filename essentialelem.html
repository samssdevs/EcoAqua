Carbon
Our body is composed of 18% carbon. Sugar, proteins, vitamins, etc. are all made up of Carbon. Carbon is also used in the medical industry to make drugs and medicines. The carbon dioxide gas we release during respiration is used by plants for the process of photosynthesis.
Essential Elements
Essential elements play key roles in various biological processes. For example, nitrogen is essential for nucleic acids and amino acids, whereas carbon serves as the structural basis of organic molecules. These particular chemical components are essential to the survival and healthy operation of living things.
Hydrogen
It is used in the process of creating water molecules which is essential for life. Hydrogen is also used to make fertilizers, ammonia, and rocket fuel that can create strong explosions.
Oxygen
This is essential for respiration, circulation, and many more physiological processes.
Sulfur
Used in the making of Sulfuric acid, insecticides, and fungicides.
Phosphorus
Used to make matches, fertilizers, Phosphorus bronze, detergents, and many more.
