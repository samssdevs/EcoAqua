Water Cycle: Collection
Is the stage of the water cycle where water is temporarily collected and stored. Collection is also known as runoff. As water returns to the Earth's surface, it will run-off into our rivers, streams, lakes, and glaciers.
What Is The Water Cycle?
The water cycle is the ongoing movement of water through Earth's atmosphere, surface, and underground. It is crucial for sustaining life, as water is a key component for plants and animals. This cycle ensures the constant distribution and replenishment of water across various ecosystems.
HOW WATER BEHAVES ON LIQUID

HOW WATER BEHAVES ON SOLID
Water turns into ice through freezing, a process where it loses heat in a freezer. As it cools, heat transfer stops particle movement, solidifying the water. To keep water in its liquid form, it should be stored at temperatures above freezing.
HOW WATER BEHAVES ON GAS
Water changes to gas through evaporation, which occurs when it is heated, such as by boiling. During this process, liquid water particles gain enough energy to overcome their attractions and become water vapor.
Water Cycle: Evaporation
The sun is the primary source of energy driving evaporation on Earth. It heats bodies of water, causing them to turn into vapor and rise into the atmosphere. This process, which occurs below water's boiling point, results in water vapor forming clouds.
Water Cycle: Condensation
The water vapor that accumulated in the atmosphere eventually cools down due to the low temperatures found at high altitudes. These vapors become tiny droplets of water and ice, eventually coming together to form clouds.
